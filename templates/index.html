Why Grid Carbon Intensity Matters

Every time you charge your laptop, boil a kettle, or run a washing machine, your carbon emissions depend directly on the active grid generation mix.

When renewable sources like wind and solar are producing high outputs, grid intensity drops significantly. If fossil fuels like gas and coal dominate, the grid becomes highly carbon-intensive.

💡 Smart Tip: Shift energy-intensive tasks (like running a dishwasher or charging an EV) to low-intensity periods to reduce your Scope 2 emissions without using less energy.

The Science & Math of Carbon Savings

Reducing carbon footprints is not abstract; it is governed by direct thermodynamics and chemical conversion rates. Below are the physical principles and math formulas that Atmo Sync uses to calculate your savings when you select actions below.

Heat Pump Efficiency (COP)

Conventional gas boilers burn fossil fuel with ~90% efficiency. Heat pumps utilize a refrigeration cycle to *move* environmental heat, achieving a Coefficient of Performance (COP) of **3.0** (300% thermal efficiency).

Savings = Gas_Usage × 0.183 - (Gas_Usage / 3.0) × 0.207
Vehicle Thermodynamic Losses

Internal combustion engines (ICE) waste ~80% of fuel energy as heat. Electric vehicles convert over **85%** of battery energy to wheel motion, drastically reducing emissions even when charging from local grids.

Savings = (Distance × 0.17 - Distance × 0.04) / 1000
Enteric Methane Fermentation

Livestock farming requires high volumes of grain inputs and directly produces methane (CH₄) via enteric fermentation. Eliminating beef and lamb cuts agricultural footprints by up to **66%**.

Savings = Diet_Baseline_Tons - Diet_Vegan_Tons

Earth’s Climate for Kids & Grownups

🌱 What is a "Carbon Footprint"?

Imagine walking across a clean green lawn with muddy boots. You leave dark mud marks wherever you step! A carbon footprint is like an invisible dirty mark we leave on the Earth. Every time we turn on a light, ride in a gasoline car, or buy a new plastic toy, we use energy and leave behind a small invisible dirty footprint in the air. We want to learn how to walk softly so our dirty footprints disappear!

☁️ The Earth's Cozy "Blanket" (Greenhouse Gases)

Our Earth is wrapped in a cozy blanket of air called gases (like carbon dioxide). This blanket traps the sun's warmth to keep us nice and warm. But when we burn coal, gas, and oil, we add extra thread to the blanket, making it **too thick**! When the blanket gets too thick, the Earth traps too much heat and gets a fever. We need to stop making the blanket thicker so our polar bears, plants, and oceans stay safe and cool!

Where Does the Smoke Come From? (Scopes)

We divide our dirty footprints into three groups depending on where the energy smoke is created:

  • 💨 Group 1 (Direct Smoke): Smoke you make yourself. For example, the gas burning inside your home boiler to make hot water, or the petrol burning inside your family car.
  • 🔌 Group 2 (Power Smoke): Smoke made when you buy power. When you turn on a light switch, your light doesn't make smoke, but a big power station far away does!
  • 🧸 Group 3 (Toy & Food Smoke): Smoke made by others to make the things you buy. Like a factory far away making a new plastic toy, or a cargo ship burning fuel to carry bananas across the ocean.

How Can Kids and Families Help?

It’s easy to help our Earth feel better! Here are simple steps you can take:

  • Turn it off: Turn off lights and TV screens when you walk out of a room.
  • Green rides: Walk, ride your bicycle, or take public transit to school instead of driving.
  • Yummy plants: Eat more delicious veggies and fruits (and eat less beef and lamb).
  • Recycle: Throw bottles, cans, and paper into the recycling bin instead of the trash!
